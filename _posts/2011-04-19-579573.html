Transneft Says Higher Dividends Would Deprive Orphans, Sick

OAO Transneft, Russia’s monopoly pipeline operator, says paying more dividends would curtail aid to orphans and other charity work.

Transneft made 3.2 billion rubles ($112 million) of charitable contributions in 2009, 7.8 times more than the dividends it paid to private investors, according to the company’s financial reports. That spurred preferred shareholders such as Prosperity Capital Management Ltd. and East Capital to demand more information as they seek a larger slice of profit.
...
“It’s a nice piece of Soviet propaganda,” said Alexander Branis, chief investment officer at Stockholm-based Prosperity, which manages about $5.6 billion of assets. “But its charter says the company’s main aim is to make money for its shareholders, not raise money for worthy causes.”
...
Transneft has a 12-month dividend yield of 0.62 percent, according to data compiled by Bloomberg. Calgary-based Pembina Pipeline Corp., which operates oil and gas pipelines in western Canada, pays 6.81 percent, and San Antonio-based NuStar Energy LP, with 8,417 miles of pipelines, pays 6.49 percent.
